Research at GLUE

Designing new approaches to solve environmental conservation and sustainable food production. We combine econometrics, statistics, data science, remote sensing, GIS and field work to uncover new information and find solutions to the challenges of human environment interactions. GLUE's current research topics include:

Tracing Commodity Supply Chains

Supply chain governance has emerged as a key approach to reduce deforestation.

U.S. Agricultural Land Use

How we use our landscapes for agriculture can either exacerbate or help mitigate the global climate and biodiversity crises.

Evaluating policy outcomes

Understanding the outcomes of policies is critical for effective governance and conservation.

Data Science for Conservation

Big data analytical techniques allow for new approaches to conserve forests and biodiversity.

Irrigation Mapping and Water Sustainability

Tracking irrigation system dynamics can improve water use and management.

Global Land and Carbon Mapping

We work to improve mapping of land and its biomass and soil carbon worldwide.

Human Health and Land Use Change

Expanding agriculture and climate change have direct impacts on human health and well-being.

Sustainable Biofuels

Bioenergy is proposed as a major pathway to meet global climate goals but can also bring risks.
